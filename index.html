Program structure — shared core + bifurcation

  • All 12 AEs together (Days 1–7): Shared core covers platform, admin, types of jobs, payments, Salesforce, and consultative selling. Items in the Lowe's column are delivered in parallel for Lowe's AEs only.

  • Split point: Account management and ongoing Lowe's pro management are not in this shared experience — they come through the Lowe's add-on and post-onboarding support.

Gaps from the six required topics

T5 — Lowe's Co-Branding Compliance Brief

The co-branding guidelines exist internally as a reference document but aren't formatted as AE training. AEs need to know before their first call: approved language ("teamed up with Lowe's"), prohibited language ("partnership"), and that incentives must always be framed as Thumbtack-driven. Note: the $300 deck is titled "Lowe's Strike Team Incentive Offer," which conflicts with the co-branding rule on incentive framing. AEs need to know how to work around that.

Action: Create a short reference card — not a full course. ~1–2 hours. Assign to Denton's team for the Lowe's add-on. Needed before Lowe's AEs go live on calls.

T5 — Preset Jobs value pitch for pros (facilitator note only)

Existing materials cover the mechanics (Teppy's guide) and the incentive offer ($300 deck), but the underlying pitch — "steady claimable jobs, no competitive bidding" — isn't developed as a standalone training moment.

Action: No new asset needed. Add a facilitator talking point to the Day 3 Activating Pros session — bridge from the Preset Jobs deep dive to "why a pro would want this."
